Fishing Report

🎯 Bite of the Day

Morning no-generation windows on Taneycomo = prime wading. Target rainbows on pink worms under a float below Fall Creek, or throw topwater (Whopper Plopper) for bass on Table Rock before the heat sets in. Morning is the magic window on both lakes.

🏞️ Table Rock Lake ★★★★☆

Bass: ★★★★★ Excellent! Summer pattern fully set — fish on offshore structure in 10–25 ft. Deep crankbaits, flutter spoons, creature baits, jigs. Topwater bite still active early with Whopper Ploppers.

Bluegill: ★★★★★ Quality fishing at season peak! 7–9" fish in 10–20 ft on gravel points and pockets. Live crickets or worms on light tackle.

Walleye: Good. Strong reproduction in recent years — James River arm showing 100% over 15". Fish or troll near deep points and channel swings.

🌡️ Water: 78.6°F 📏 Level: 916.92 ft 💧 Clarity: Clear main lake, stained river arms

🌊 Lake Taneycomo ★★★★☆

Rainbow Trout: Good to Very Good — fishing has improved over the past two weeks. Morning no-generation windows (water off until ~1–2 PM) give solid wading opportunity. Pink worm under float below Fall Creek is the #1 summer bait. 40+ fish days common for those who adapt to flow.

Hot spot: Branson Landing area — stocked rainbows schooled up and eager to eat. Spoon throwing from the pier dock near Turkey Creek producing on nearly every cast. Some 19.5" trophies reported this week!

🌡️ Water: 50°F 💧 Clarity: Very clear 📈 Afternoon generation: up to 12,800 cfs ⚠️ Check SWPA schedule before wading
💡 Pro Tip

2-lb Test Line is Critical

Taneycomo water is very clear right now. If bites are slow, tie on 2-lb test (or 6x tippet for fly anglers). Light line makes a real difference. No felt-soled wading boots (banned in Missouri).
