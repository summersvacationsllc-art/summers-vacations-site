🎣 Table Rock Lake

🌡 76–80°F 📏 917.0 ft (2 ft above full pool) 💧 Stained to clear
🐟 Bass Excellent 6–25 ft
🐟 Walleye Excellent Deep gravel points
🐟 Crappie Fair 10–15 ft
🐟 Bluegill Peak 4–10 ft
🐟 Catfish Good 3–15 ft

🎯 Techniques

Bass: Whopper Plopper at dawn, drop shot (live nightcrawlers 15–20 ft), Carolina rig (green pumpkin worms 6–18 ft). Jigs in shoreline bushes. Walleye: Drop shot with nightcrawler on extended main lake points. Bluegill: Live nightcrawlers near docks and brush. Catfish: Cut bait near flat points, early morning or late evening.

🎣 Lake Taneycomo

🌡 ~50°F year-round 💧 Very clear ⚡ Generation: Off mornings → ~12,800 cfs after 1 PM
🎣 Trout Good

🔥 Hot Baits

Pink worm under a float — the standout bait. Also: jig under a float, mega worm (cream/peach), nightcrawlers, drifting eggs, PowerBait (chartreuse/pink). Fly fishing: zebra midge, scud, olive Wooly Bugger, Tiger Tail.

📍 Hot Spots

Branson Landing (current hot spot), pier dock across from Scotty's Dock, between Fall Creek and Cooper Creek, Trophy Area below Fall Creek to dam, MDC Boat Dock.

📌 Key Tips

Get out early — water off until 1–2 PM. Best window 5:30 AM–noon. Use 2 lb test line. Clean presentations critical. Check generation before wading. USA Bassin tournament on Taneycomo today — extra boat traffic expected.
